How to Update Your Email Signature

After copying your signature using the appropriate button above, follow the steps for your email client:

Gmail
  1. Open Gmail in your web browser.
  2. Click the Settings gear icon (⚙️) in the top right, then click "See all settings".
  3. Scroll down to the "Signature" section.
  4. Click "+ Create new" or Edit (pencil) an existing one.
  5. Give your signature a name.
  6. In the signature editing box, delete any existing content.
  7. Paste your copied signature (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V). It should appear formatted.
  8. Select the signature for New emails / Replies below.
  9. Scroll to the bottom and click "Save Changes".
Outlook (Desktop App)
  1. Open Outlook.
  2. Go to File > Options (Windows) or Outlook > Preferences > Signatures (Mac).
  3. Select Mail > Signatures... (Windows).
  4. Select account, click New or select existing.
  5. Give it a name.
  6. In the "Edit signature" box, delete existing content.
  7. Paste your copied signature (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V). Use the 'Copy for Outlook' button for best results.
  8. Choose defaults for New messages and optionally for Replies/forwards.
  9. Click OK.

Note: If pasting doesn't look right, some Outlook versions allow pasting HTML directly (look for an option or try right-clicking).

Apple Mail (macOS)
  1. Open Mail app.
  2. Go to Mail > Preferences... (or Settings...).
  3. Click Signatures tab.
  4. Select account, click "+".
  5. Give it a name.
  6. Uncheck "Always match my default message font".
  7. Delete placeholder text.
  8. Paste your copied signature (Cmd+V). Use the 'Copy for Apple Mail' button.
  9. Assign signature to account (drag or use the "Choose Signature" dropdown).
  10. Close Preferences/Settings.
